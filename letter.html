Letter to Shareholders

In 2021, our company continued to successfully execute our strategy and achieve strong operational performance. We are proud of our employees for their commitment to keeping our workplace and the communities where we operate safe throughout the COVID-19 pandemic

Our strategy has been and continues to be to deliver high return resource growth, a low cost of supply and industry leading cash flow growth. We have built a differentiated portfolio offering a unique value proposition – delivering durable cash flow growth that enables us to invest in some of the highest return projects in the industry and also to grow cash returns to our shareholders.

In terms of resource growth, we have been disciplined in allocating capital to the best rocks for the best returns. Our portfolio is focused on the Bakken, the deepwater Gulf of Mexico, Southeast Asia and Guyana, with its multiple phases of low cost oil developments, and we expect all four of these assets to be free cash flow generative in 2022. Our 2022 capital and exploratory budget is $2.6 billion, of which approximately 80% will be allocated to Guyana and the Bakken.

Because of the investments we are making, we are well positioned to significantly lower our cost of supply. By 2026, our cash costs are forecast to decline approximately 25% compared with 2021 to $9 per barrel of oil equivalent. In addition, our portfolio’s breakeven oil price is expected to be one of the lowest in the industry by 2026, decreasing to $45 per barrel Brent.

In terms of cash flow growth, we have an industry leading rate of change and durability story. Our company is positioned to grow our cash flow at a compound rate of 25% per year out to 2026 based on a Brent oil price of $65 per barrel.

Our second offshore development in Guyana, Liza Phase 2, began producing oil in February 2022 and, at capacity, is expected to add more than $1 billion of net operating cash flow annually at $65 per barrel Brent. Following startup, we repaid the remaining $500 million of our term loan, and in March 2022, we announced a 50% increase in our quarterly dividend. As our portfolio becomes increasingly free cash flow positive in the coming years, we commit to return up to 75% of our adjusted free cash flow annually to shareholders by continuing to grow the dividend and accelerating share repurchases.

Key to our strategy is Guyana, the largest new oil province discovered in the last decade, which is positioned to be one of the highest margin, lowest carbon intensity oil developments in the world according to Wood Mackenzie data. On the Stabroek Block, where Hess has a 30% interest and ExxonMobil is the operator, we continued our extraordinary string of exploration successes with five significant discoveries in 2021 and two additional significant discoveries in January 2022. Our four sanctioned oil developments on the block have world class economics, with a Brent breakeven oil price of between $25 and $35 per barrel. In 2027, at least six floating production, storage and offloading vessels (FPSOs) are expected to be online on the Stabroek Block with a production capacity of more than 1 million gross barrels of oil per day, with the potential for up to 10 FPSOs to develop the gross discovered recoverable resources of more than 10 billion barrels of oil equivalent.

The Bakken, our largest operated asset, remains an important part of our portfolio with a robust inventory of high return future drilling locations. In 2021, with continued strength in oil prices, we added a second and third rig, which will enable us to accelerate cash flow growth, lower our unit cash costs and further optimize our infrastructure.

Turning to our 2021 financial results, our adjusted net income was $677 million compared with an adjusted net loss of $894 million in 2020, primarily due to higher realized prices. Cash flow from operations, before changes in working capital, was $3.0 billion, compared with $1.8 billion in the prior year. Sustainability

As we continue to execute our strategy, our company will be guided by our longstanding commitment to sustainability. Our strategy aligns with the world’s growing need for affordable, reliable and cleaner energy that is necessary for human prosperity and global economic development. We recognize that climate change is the greatest scientific challenge of the 21st century and support the aim of the Paris Agreement and a global ambition to achieve net zero emissions by 2050. Our business planning includes actions we will undertake to continue reducing our carbon footprint.

Our Board of Directors is climate change literate and actively engaged in overseeing Hess’ sustainability practices. We are committed to transparency – our strategy and reporting are closely aligned with the recommendations of the Task Force on Climate-Related Financial Disclosures.

Our Board and senior leadership have set aggressive targets for greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions reduction. After our company significantly outperformed our five year emissions reduction targets for 2020, we announced new five year GHG reduction targets for 2025, which are to reduce operated Scope 1 and 2 GHG emissions intensity and methane emissions intensity by approximately 50% from 2017 and to eliminate routine flaring from our operations by the end of 2025. In addition, we are contributing to groundbreaking work by the Salk Institute to develop plants with larger root systems that are capable of absorbing and storing potentially billions of tons of carbon per year from the atmosphere.

We have tested the resilience of Hess’ portfolio under the supply and demand scenarios from the International Energy Agency (IEA). In the IEA’s Sustainable Development Scenario, which assumes all the pledges of the Paris Agreement are met, oil and gas will still be 40% of the global energy mix in 2040. In the IEA’s newest Net Zero Scenario, oil and gas will still be 29% of the energy mix in 2040. In either scenario, oil and gas will be needed for decades to come. Hess’ strategic priorities – to grow our resource base, have a low cost of supply and sustain cash flow growth – are aligned with the energy transition needed to achieve the IEA’s scenarios and position us well for the coming decades.

We are honored to have been recognized throughout 2021 as an industry leader in our environmental, social and governance performance and disclosure. In keeping with our Hess Values, we are committed to diversity, equity and inclusion, which we believe creates value for all of our stakeholders, and to the safety of our workforce and the communities where we operate. A multidisciplinary team continues to update our COVID-19 prevention protocols based on current conditions and guidance from public health authorities and our medical advisors.
