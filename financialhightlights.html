Financial and Operating Highlights

Production

In 2021, net production averaged 315,000 barrels of oil equivalent per day, including Libya, compared with 331,000 barrels of oil equivalent per day in 2020. The year over year decrease was primarily the result of lower Bakken production and the sale of our interests in the Shenzi Field in the Gulf of Mexico in 2020 and the South Arne Field in Denmark in 2021, which was partially offset by higher production from the Liza Phase 1 development in Guyana and more stable operations in Libya.

In the Bakken, Hess’ operated rig count averaged 2.3 in 2021, compared with 2.6 in 2020. As West Texas Intermediate oil prices moved above $50 per barrel, the company added a second operated rig in February 2021 and a third operated rig in September 2021. We brought 51 new wells on production in 2021, compared with 111 wells in 2020. Net production from the Bakken averaged 156,000 barrels of oil equivalent per day in 2021, compared with 193,000 barrels of oil equivalent per day in 2020. The decrease was due to a lower average rig count, higher prices for natural gas liquids that increased the company’s earnings and cash flow but lowered production entitlements under the company’s percentage of proceeds contracts, the planned turnaround of the Tioga Gas Plant, and the sale of the company’s Little Knife and Murphy Creek nonstrategic acreage interests.

Developments

Offshore Guyana, Hess holds a 30% interest in the 6.6 million acre Stabroek Block. Esso Exploration and Production Guyana Limited, a subsidiary of ExxonMobil, is operator and holds a 45% interest. CNOOC Petroleum Guyana Limited, a wholly owned subsidiary of CNOOC Limited, holds the remaining 25% interest.

First oil from the Liza Phase 2 development was achieved in February 2022, utilizing the Liza Unity floating production, storage and offloading vessel with production capacity of approximately 220,000 gross barrels of oil per day. A third development at the Payara Field, also with production capacity of 220,000 gross barrels of oil per day, was sanctioned in 2020 with first oil expected in 2024. Our three sanctioned oil developments on the block have a Brent breakeven oil price of between $25 and $35 per barrel.
